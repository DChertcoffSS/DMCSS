U.S. Government Solicitations: Structure and Process

Prepared by David Chertcoff · DMC Service Solutions

This post summarizes how U.S. Government solicitations are structured (Sections A–M), outlines key steps in the acquisition process, and highlights practical FAR compliance tips. Use it as a quick reference when preparing proposals or advising capture teams.

Solicitation Structure (Sections A–M)

  • A — Information to Offerors
  • B — Supplies or Services and Price/Cost
  • C — Statement of Work
  • D — Packaging and Marking
  • E — Inspection or Acceptance
  • F — Deliveries or Performance
  • G — Contract Administration Data
  • H — Special Contract Requirement
  • I — Contract Clauses
  • J — Attachments and Exhibits
  • K — Representations and Certifications
  • L — Instructions to Offerors

Key Steps in the Process

  1. Need Identification — Agencies define requirements and objectives.
  2. Pre‑Solicitation Planning — Market research and acquisition strategy.
  3. Solicitation Issuance — RFPs or Invitations released.
  4. Proposal Submission — Vendors respond with detailed offers.
  5. Evaluation & Award — Price, technical capability, and past performance assessed.
  6. Contract Administration — Performance management and compliance.
  7. Closeout — Obligations completed; contract formally closed.

FAR Compliance Tips

  • Read Sections L & M carefully; tailor to instructions and evaluation criteria.
  • Ensure cost realism and internal consistency across volumes.
